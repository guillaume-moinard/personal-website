Can we do science with protests ?

A web site to present simulations of agents aggregating on city graphs inspired from Extinction Rebellion actions, and all sorts of ideas on protests.

About me

I am a PhD student in computer science at Sorbonne University. My main focus is computational social science, espacially complex systems such as crowds on city networks. For the moment I focus on crowds dynamics in cities, using tools from graph theory, game theory and statistical physics.

But first, what is a graph ?

A graph is a pair G = (V, E), where V is a set whose elements are called vertices, and E is a set of paired vertices, whose elements are called edges. In our case, vertices represent intersections of the city road network, and an edge is a street linking two intersections.

Some more details

We started this work as no one seems to have studied protests from the point of view of a game theory, with strategies, on a planar graph representing the city. Below I leave you a few informations on our work and my reseach. Future documentation will be implemented to use our work.

Data

Our city graphs are generated with data from OpenStreeMap. Details on the maps can be added with opendataParis.

Technical issues

My work is still in progress. Articles have still not been published, and bugs can be found in the simulations. Patience friends, we will improve.

Gaming

What about doing science and having fun ? This is participative science ! We intend to build a game-like interface, allowing people to test different stragtegies to evaluate their impacts.
